The Reading Room smelled of old light and cold stone, the kind of cold that seeps up through centuries of floor rather than through any window. Mira Halloway pressed her fingertips against the spine of the nearest shelf and felt the words hum back at her — not sound exactly, but pressure, meaning transferred through glass the way warmth transfers through cupped palms. She had been an apprentice for three weeks and still could not read more than a sentence before the letters slipped away like fish beneath dark water. Custodian Bell watched from the archway, its brass face expressionless, its key-wound heart ticking out the seconds with the patience of something that had no reason to hurry.
