The door at the end of the restricted stacks was not locked by any mechanism Mira could identify — no keyhole, no latch, only a panel of dark glass set flush into the iron frame and etched with a single sentence she could not yet read. She pressed her palm flat against it anyway, and for a moment something on the other side pressed back. Custodian Bell appeared at her shoulder without footsteps, the way it always did, and said in its careful measured voice that the Compact permitted curiosity but not entrance, not yet, not until the ink in her fingers had learned to keep a secret.
