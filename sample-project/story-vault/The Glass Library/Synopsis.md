# The Glass Library — Synopsis

Mira Halloway is a young apprentice librarian with ink-stained fingers and an unusual talent: she can feel meaning in glass-script a heartbeat longer than any apprentice before her. When she is posted to the Glass Library in the world of Argent, she expects cataloging work. What she finds instead is Custodian Bell — an ancient clockwork construct who has outlasted every librarian the Compact ever sent — and a locked door at the end of the restricted stacks that has no entry in any index.

As Mira's ability to read glass-script deepens, she begins to understand that the library is not merely a repository. It is a story being written in real time, and the locked door leads to its ending. Bell has protected that ending for centuries, not to keep readers out, but to keep the ending in — because the final page, once read, rewrites the reader.

The Glass Library is a quiet fantasy about knowledge, patience, and the question of whether understanding something fully means becoming it.
