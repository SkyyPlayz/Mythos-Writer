# The Glass Library — Working Notes

- The glass-script reading experience should feel tactile and slightly uncanny — warmth, pressure, the sense of meaning arriving before language does. Avoid making it feel digital or telepathic.
- Bell should never lie directly. Every evasion should be technically true. This matters for the ending.
- The Compact auditor is not an antagonist — they are doing their job competently and in good faith. The tension they create should come from the situation, not from their character.
- Consider: what does the library smell like? Cold stone, yes, but also something else. The accumulated touch of many readers. Something almost organic.
- The locked door inscription — draft: "What is written here was written for the last reader." Need to verify this works as a glass-script sentence that reads back.
- Mira's ink-stained fingers are not purely aesthetic. The Compact marks its licensed readers with a compound that improves glass-script sensitivity. Mira has more of it than is standard for a three-week apprentice.
