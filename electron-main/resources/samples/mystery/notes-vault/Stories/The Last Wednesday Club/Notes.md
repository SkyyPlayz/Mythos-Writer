# Working Notes — The Last Wednesday Club

## Voice
Precise, observant, restrained. Helena's POV is that of a close reader who applies the same methods to people that she applied to texts: attention to what is there, what is absent, what doesn't fit the pattern. The prose should be clean and slightly cool without being cold.

## Helena's Relationship to Deduction
Helena is not a natural detective in the sense of enjoying the puzzle. She investigates because Oliver is dead and he trusted her — not with the mystery but with his time, his friendship, his Wednesday evenings. She feels it as an obligation, not a pleasure.

## Constable Whittaker
Whittaker is competent and initially condescending, but he updates his view of Helena quickly and is a genuine ally from Scene 2 onward. He is not comic relief; he is what a decent local constable looks like when he's working hard.

## The Book Club as Structure
*The Comfort of Strangers* — the novel they were going to discuss — is about violence between people who know each other and the gap between surface and reality. Oliver chose it for December. This was not accidental.
