# Story Beats — The Last Wednesday Club

## Act 1: The Discovery
- Oliver's empty chair; Helena's methodical observation of the scene
- The two wine glasses establish that something is wrong
- Helena's thirty-one-year knowledge of Oliver is the investigation's foundation

## Act 2: The Web
- Oliver's papers; the years 1995-96 as the key period
- Beatrice's careful management of information — she knows more than she shares
- Frederick's wrong chair and wrong behaviour at the book club
- Sonia's arrival changes what's possible: she has information Helena couldn't access

## Act 3: The Document
- Oliver planned for this; the document is the mystery's resolution
- The moment of clarity: Helena understands that Oliver knew things might go wrong and built in a failsafe
- Beatrice and Frederick are exposed by Oliver's own careful work, not by Helena's cleverness

## Key Structural Note
Helena is not the hero who outwits the villain. She is the person who creates the conditions for Oliver's own intelligence to work posthumously. The mystery is about Oliver's foresight as much as Helena's deduction. He left the evidence for her to find.
