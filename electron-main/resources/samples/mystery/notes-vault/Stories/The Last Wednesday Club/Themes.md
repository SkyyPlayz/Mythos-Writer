# Themes — The Last Wednesday Club

**What we owe the dead**: Oliver spent his last day making sure the truth would come out. Helena's role is to honour that work by not letting it be buried. The debt to the dead is a form of loyalty.

**Long friendship and its limits**: The book club has run for thirty-one years; the friendships in it are real and layered. The horror of the story is partly that Beatrice — a genuinely good person in many dimensions — participated in something terrible partly because of loyalty to Frederick.

**Truth vs. comfort**: Detective fiction's underlying question. The Wednesday Club discusses books that make their members uncomfortable; it is also, it turns out, a community that has been sheltering a truth that needed to be uncomfortable.

**The prepared mind**: Helena's reading life gave her the instinct to observe without concluding. Oliver's legal training gave him the instinct to document everything. Both are forms of preparation for exactly this situation.
