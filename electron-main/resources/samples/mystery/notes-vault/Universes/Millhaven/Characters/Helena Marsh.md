# Helena Marsh

Retired English teacher, sixty-seven, thirty-one-year member of the Wednesday Club and its de facto intellectual anchor. She taught for thirty-eight years, specialised in the twentieth-century novel, and has read more detective fiction than she would admit to in a literary context. She has a methodical mind — she reads people the way she reads texts, looking for what the surface arrangement reveals about the underlying structure — and a characteristic patience that she has sometimes confused with passivity. She was widowed twelve years ago and has been building, slowly, a life that is entirely her own. She came to Millhaven originally for her husband; she stayed because she couldn't find a reason to leave and eventually found reasons to stay.

**Arc:** From grieving friend to active investigator; discovering that the instinct she'd always attributed to reading turns out to have practical application.
