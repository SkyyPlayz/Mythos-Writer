# Dr. Frederick Tam

Millhaven's GP for thirty-two years. He is sixty-one, thoughtful, genuinely well-liked, and has been managing the weight of a financial crime committed in 1995 for three decades. He mismanaged a patient's estate for personal gain — not out of malice but out of opportunism and a young man's confidence that he would get away with it — and has been, in the years since, an excellent doctor in part because medicine is how he justifies the rest of his history to himself. He came to the book club meeting on the evening Oliver died with enough presence of mind to choose the wrong chair and keep his coat on. He is not, fundamentally, a violent person. Whether that characterisation survives what the story reveals about him is one of its central questions.

**Role in story:** The architect of the fraud; whether he caused Oliver's death directly or was present when it happened is the question the toxicology report will answer.
