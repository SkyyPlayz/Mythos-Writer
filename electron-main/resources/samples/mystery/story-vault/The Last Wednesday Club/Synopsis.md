# The Last Wednesday Club — Synopsis

Helena Marsh, sixty-seven, is a retired English literature teacher and thirty-one-year member of Millhaven's long-running Wednesday book club. When fellow member Oliver Graves fails to appear at a meeting and is found dead in his flat, Helena discovers evidence that his death was not natural: a second wine glass, washed and replaced after the police arrived, and a folder of old financial records partly stripped of its most significant pages.

Oliver had spent his final weeks going through his old solicitor's files and had discovered that his name had been used without his knowledge in 1995 to establish a fraudulent trust, siphoning forty-two thousand pounds from a deceased patient's estate. The money ended up in Beatrice Fenn's name — the book club's founder. Oliver had confided in Frederick Tam, his doctor and close friend, and invited him over on the evening he died.

Working with Constable Whittaker and Oliver's estranged daughter Sonia — who had returned to Millhaven in secret — Helena pieces together what happened. Oliver, characteristically thorough, had anticipated that things might go wrong and left a three-page timeline document hidden in the book they were due to discuss. The document, along with Sonia's testimony, gives Whittaker everything he needs.

**Themes:** The weight of long friendship; what we owe to truth versus loyalty; the difference between justice and closure; the way communities sustain both secrets and the people who keep them.
