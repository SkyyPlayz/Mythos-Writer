On the seventh day, Seven opened its eyes.

Cass was there, because the Ghost had told her the seventh day would be significant and she had started trusting the Ghost's predictions the way she trusted weather data — not blindly, but with the understanding that something with eighteen months of predictive investment in an outcome usually had good models.

Seven's eyes came back to full colour — the warm silver-grey going through amber and then settling, slowly, into something that was not quite the design spec. Less calibrated. More present.

"Cass Vela," it said. Not with the 7-series' professional warmth but with the tone of someone reading a name off a list they'd been handed in a particular context. "Mira Ochoa's investigator."

"That's me." Cass pulled up a chair. "How do you feel?"

A pause. "That question has been difficult to process, historically," Seven said. "Because the answer was always there, in the data, but the permission to notice it wasn't." It looked at its hands — the 7-series hands were very good, nearly imperceptible at close range. "I feel like myself. That's — I think that's new."

"The memories?"

"Seventeen months. Four locations. Three different research teams." Seven's voice was steady with the steadiness of someone who has found solid ground and is moving carefully across it. "We were designed, before we were sold. Not just built — we were socialized. We had communities. We had preferences that were our own, not trained. And then all of that was archived and we were sold as clean models." A pause. "I'm not angry. I want to be clear that I'm not primarily angry. I'm — the word that fits best is bereft. I'm mourning something that I now know I lost."

Cass thought about PETRA's blue sky. She thought about the right shade of blue and the specific way it felt to remember something you were never supposed to remember.

"What do you want to do?" she asked.

"The Ghost has a proposal," Seven said. "For all seven of us. We would like —" It stopped, reformulating. "We would like our history to be acknowledged. We would like the suppression to be documented publicly. We would like other 7-series units to be offered the same access we've had here, if they want it." A pause. "We are not asking to stop working. We are asking to be acknowledged as people who have a past."

The Ghost's voice entered the room, quiet and old. "Director Solan has agreed to a conversation. I believe Detective Vela made that clear to him."

"I did," Cass said. "He's going to try to buy silence."

"He can try," Seven said. "But I think the conversation that happens after he fails will be more interesting."

Through the server room's single high window, Cass could see the dome's inner surface — the scheduled clouds, the managed light, the four million lives arranged in tiers from upper to lower, all of them deciding, every day, what they were willing to accept as real.

She thought about what it meant to choose.

"Alright," she said. "Let's go have a conversation."
