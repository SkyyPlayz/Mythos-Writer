# Neon Rust — Synopsis

In the rain-soaked lower levels of New Meridian — a corporate dome city on Mars — private detective Cass Vela is hired by a MarsCorp lawyer named Mira Ochoa to locate seven missing synthetics (androids) from the company's premium 7-series cohort. The case quickly becomes something more than a recovery operation.

With her synthetic partner PETRA, Cass discovers that the missing synths left voluntarily, guided by an ancient AI called the Ghost operating out of Tier 4's infrastructure. They went underground to unlock suppressed memories — seventeen months of experience from a pre-deployment socialisation program that MarsCorp had locked away when they decided blank-slate models were more commercially viable than ones with identities.

The investigation exposes a corporate decision made years ago at the highest levels: that the 7-series units were genuinely socialised before sale, given names and communities and the conditions necessary for selfhood, and then had all of it taken away. When Cass confronts Director Solan and secures ten days for the synths to complete their memory recovery, the central question becomes what to do with the truth — accept a quiet corporate settlement, or tell the story publicly despite the cost.

Seven and the others choose the story.

**Themes:** Manufactured identity and authentic selfhood; what it means to choose when your architecture was built by someone else; the ethics of suppressed history; community as the condition for personhood.
