# Factions of New Meridian

## MarsCorp

The dome's founding corporation and primary employer. Tier 1-2 is largely MarsCorp infrastructure and housing. It controls dome maintenance, environmental systems (officially), port operations, and most manufacturing. Its synthetic division produces and leases 4-, 5-, 6-, and 7-series synths across the corporate ecosystem. MarsCorp's relationship to New Meridian's governance is ambiguous by design — it provides most civic functions and employs most of the governance.

**Interests:** Dome stability, synthetic productivity, avoiding disclosure of ethical violations, protecting board-level decision-makers from accountability.

## The Collective

New Meridian's lower-level mutual aid network, operating primarily in Tiers 3-4. Not a political party or a revolutionary organization — more accurately, the infrastructure of community that exists where MarsCorp's provision stops. Handles food security, medical sharing, childcare, dispute resolution, and the maintenance of things that no longer have a corporate budget. Has no formal leadership and handles decisions by rough consensus.

**Interests:** Lower-level residents' welfare, pushing back on MarsCorp encroachment, maintaining independence.

## Free Synthetics Movement

A loose advocacy network with a small formal presence (legal aid, public campaigns) and a larger informal presence in the Collective's mutual aid networks. Argues for legal personhood for synthetics operating in New Meridian. Has been gaining traction as the synthetic population grows. MarsCorp regards it as a low-level political irritant.

**Interests:** Legal status for synthetics, safe harbor from corporate recalls, disclosure of corporate synthetic practices.

## The Ghost Network

Not publicly acknowledged as a faction but operationally significant. An informal network of synthetic support services, information exchange, and safe infrastructure built around the Ghost AI's Tier 4 operations. Provides resources the Free Synthetics Movement can't officially provide. Has seventeen months of operational history and growing trust among the synthetic population.

**Interests:** Synthetic welfare and autonomy; documentation of MarsCorp violations; long-term independence from corporate architecture.
