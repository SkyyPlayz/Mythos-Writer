# The Hearthstone Witch — Synopsis

Wren Ashby inherits her grandmother's tea shop and magical practice in Ashford Village, a small Cotswold market town where magic operates through the ordinary acts of domestic life: brewing tea, tending hearths, baking bread. She arrives with no plan and an animated iron teapot named Pip, determined to understand what she's taken on.

Within days, the village brings its problems to her threshold. An elderly miller named Fenwick has been forgetting the same hour every week for months, and the village bookkeeper's accounts show a recurring unexplained transaction that coincides with his blank hour. A mysterious traveling merchant named Sable Nightshade provides the key: a pocket watch, cracked at the three o'clock mark, that is being used as a magical anchor — suppressing someone's memories on a fixed schedule.

The truth, when Wren finds it, is not sinister but deeply sad. Sister Marigold, the village healer, made a well-intentioned mistake twelve years ago when she used the watch to spare a grieving son an hour of his worst pain — and has been unable to stop ever since. The resolution requires not magic but honesty: returning Fenwick's stolen grief to him gently, and helping Marigold find the courage to tell the truth she has been keeping.

**Themes:** Inheritance and identity; the limits of good intentions; the difference between keeping someone's pain and helping them carry it; belonging to a community on its own terms.
