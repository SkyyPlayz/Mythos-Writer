# The Hearthstone Witch — Outline

## Chapter 1: A Cup of Something Warm

**Scene 1 — The Inheritance**
Wren collects her grandmother's key from the solicitor, meets Pip, reads the letter. Establishes the shop, the magic system, the village context, and Wren's character.

**Scene 2 — First Customers**
Elder Mossfoot arrives before the shop officially opens and introduces the central problem: Old Fenwick forgets the same hour every week. Wren agrees to look into it.

**Scene 3 — The Broken Kettle**
Delphine Coldwater arrives skeptically seeking help with a dead sourdough culture. Wren assists and begins to understand the scope of her role in the village — not just spells but warmth.

## Chapter 2: The Broken Clock

**Scene 1 — Old Fenwick's Problem**
Wren meets Fenwick himself. She can sense the gap in his resonance — a door that has been locked from the outside.

**Scene 2 — Market Day**
Saturday market. Thessaly Briarbend shares the financial anomaly she's noticed in Fenwick's accounts. Wren spots Sable Nightshade for the first time.

**Scene 3 — Midnight Consultation**
Nightshade visits after midnight. Reveals the pocket watch, offers a theory about the compulsion's mechanism, begins an uneasy alliance.

## Chapter 3: What the Hearth Remembers

**Scene 1 — Grandmother's Secret**
Wren sits still long enough to receive a memory from the hearth — her grandmother watching Sister Marigold wind the watch.

**Scene 2 — The Resolution**
Wren confronts Marigold at the mill. Learns the truth of the original intention and its twelve-year drift into harm. Begins the slow, careful work of giving Fenwick's grief back to him.
