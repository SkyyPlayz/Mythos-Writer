# Story Beats — The Hearthstone Witch

## Act 1: Arrival
- Wren receives the inheritance, arrives at the shop, meets Pip
- Village introduces itself via Elder Mossfoot (the Fenwick problem)
- Wren demonstrates competence and warmth; establishes her as worthy of the role

## Act 2: Investigation
- Fenwick: the gap in the resonance field, the repeating forgotten hour
- Thessaly: financial anomaly maps to Thursday 3pm
- Nightshade: the watch, the compulsion mechanism, the alliance
- Market day: the village as a web of relationships Wren is entering

## Act 3: Truth and Resolution
- Hearth-memory reveals Marigold
- Confrontation at the mill: not accusation but invitation to honesty
- Resolution: grief returned gently; Marigold makes repair
- Wren is fully the shop's keeper now, no longer temporary

## Key Beat: Grandmother's Presence
The grandmother should feel present throughout — in the notes, the chalk-board, the fire — but her appearances should cost Wren something (requiring stillness, attention, letting go of the urgency to act). The final chalk-board message is the graduation.
