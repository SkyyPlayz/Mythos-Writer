# Themes — The Hearthstone Witch

**Inheritance as conversation**: What we receive from those who came before us is not a set of instructions but an ongoing dialogue. Wren's task is to understand her grandmother's choices, not merely to replicate them.

**Magic as care**: The Hearth Compact's magic only works through genuine warmth. This is the story's central argument: that attention, presence, and care are not merely nice-to-have qualities but a source of actual power.

**Good intentions are not enough**: Marigold's story demonstrates that well-meaning intervention without consent has costs that compound over time. The resolution is not punishment but accountability and repair.

**Belonging is earned and given**: Wren doesn't become the village witch by inheriting the shop; she becomes it by choosing, over and over, to show up for the people who live there.
