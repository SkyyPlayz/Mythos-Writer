# Juniper Stonewall

Ashford's village constable is forty-one, has been in post for twelve years, and has the particular patience of someone who has seen enough small-community problems to know that most of them involve hurt feelings and most of those involve someone not asking for what they actually need. He is professionally skeptical of the tea shop but personally respectful of results, and he came in on day two to introduce himself and assess Pip with the careful attention of a man who knows an instrument of civic disruption when he sees one. He is unmarried, a reliable cook, and slightly too fond of procedural fairness.

**Role in story:** Community authority figure; provides legal and practical context; evolving relationship with Wren's practice.
