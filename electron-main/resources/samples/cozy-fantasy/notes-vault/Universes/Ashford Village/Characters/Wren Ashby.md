# Wren Ashby

Wren is twenty-six, bookish, quietly stubborn, and deeply unprepared to be a witch. She came to Ashford on two weeks' notice after a Scottish graduate scholarship she'd deferred twice, having never quite managed to stay away from her grandmother's letters long enough to become the academic she'd planned to be. She has her grandmother's green eyes and her father's talent for finding the most complicated possible interpretation of a simple problem. She makes good tea by instinct, reads people well by necessity, and is slowly learning that the domestic magic of the Hearth Compact is not a system to be studied so much as a way of being present in the world. Pip exasperates her and she is devoted to it.

**Role in story:** Protagonist and viewpoint character. Inherits the shop, the practice, and the ongoing mystery of Fenwick's stolen hour.

**Arc:** From competent outsider to rooted community member. Learning the difference between solving problems and holding space for people to solve their own.
