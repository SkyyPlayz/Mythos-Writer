# Captain Aldric Vane

The sea captain passes through Ashford in the autumn, heading inland for reasons he doesn't fully explain, carrying cargo he is even less forthcoming about. He is fifty-three, sun-darkened, with the deliberate unhurriedness of someone who has been in enough emergencies to know that panic is a resource drain. He came to the tea shop looking for directions and stayed for two cups because Pip, uncharacteristically, sat on the table and watched him with every sign of fascination. He knows things about the northern coastal routes that may become significant later.

**Role in story:** Secondary character with potential future importance; represents the wider world beyond Ashford.
