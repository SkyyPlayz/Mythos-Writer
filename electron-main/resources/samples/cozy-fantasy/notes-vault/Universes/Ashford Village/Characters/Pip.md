# Pip

Pip is a small iron teapot with a dented lid, a spout that points things out with uncanny precision, and opinions about everything. It has been in the Ashby family for four generations and has developed the personality of something that has seen a great deal and judges most of it. It communicates through orientation (spout as compass, indicating attention), temperature (warmer for trust, cooler for concern), and the particular vocabulary of kettle-sounds — a small art form Wren is learning to read. Pip does not speak. It does not need to. It has the magnificent self-assurance of a creature that has never once doubted its own perceptions.

**Role in story:** Wren's familiar and constant companion; provides guidance, commentary, and emotional grounding.
