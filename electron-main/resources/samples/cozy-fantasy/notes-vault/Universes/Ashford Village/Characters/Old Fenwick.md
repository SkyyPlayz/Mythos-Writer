# Old Fenwick

Conal Fenwick is sixty-seven and has run the family mill for thirty years. He is warm, cheerful, precise about grain and weather and water levels, and completely unaware that he has been losing an hour every Thursday for twelve years. The missing time has shaped him in ways he can't see: he is occasionally bewildered by his own sense that something is missing, and he has developed a habit of watching the clock between two and four in the afternoon with a vague, unfocused sadness he attributes to getting older. He loved his mother deeply and lost her well.

**Role in story:** The mystery's victim; his stolen grief is the case Wren is solving.
