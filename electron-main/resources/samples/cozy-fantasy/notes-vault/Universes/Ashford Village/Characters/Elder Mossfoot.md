# Elder Mossfoot

The elder of Ashford Village in every sense: longest resident, most connections, deepest memory of how things work and why. He is seventy-eight, compact and durable as old oak, with a deliberate manner that younger residents sometimes mistake for slowness. He came to Wren's grandmother with every problem the village couldn't solve by itself, and he has transferred this trust to Wren with the pragmatic efficiency of a man who knows what resources a village needs and doesn't waste time on ceremony. He lost his wife four years ago and carries it the way he carries everything — steadily, without display.

**Role in story:** First client; introduces the Fenwick problem; serves as Wren's informal guide to village dynamics.
