# The Hearth Compact

The Hearth Compact is the name given to both the governing body of domestic magic practitioners and the underlying system they draw on. It is old — older than the villages it mostly inhabits — and operates on a principle that takes most new practitioners some time to accept: that magic is not a separate force channelled through exceptional people, but a quality inherent in care itself.

Hearth Compact magic works through domestic acts performed with full attention and intention: brewing tea, baking bread, lighting fires, weaving cloth, sweeping floors. The practitioner does not impose the magic — they invite it. The warmth must be genuine. Performed warmth, care without feeling, generates no resonance at all. This is why the Compact has no ceremony of initiation: you cannot fake your way into it.

The system has its own ethical gravity. It resists manipulation and compulsion, though both are possible with sufficient skill and the wrong intentions. Objects made or maintained within strong domestic magic — hearthstones, kitchen tools, inherited items — can hold resonance for generations. Familiars (animate companions like Pip) develop in close proximity to practitioners and are not created so much as *recognised*.

The governing Compact is informal: practitioners know each other, share knowledge through networks of apprenticeship and correspondence, and maintain the rule that magic used against a person's knowledge or will is cause for community intervention.
